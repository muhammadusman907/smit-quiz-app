When using single-stage systems at conditions below 0 Â°F, the two main problems encountered are:

  • High discharge temperatures, and
  • Reduced compressor refrigeration capacity.

Both situations are a result of the high compression ratios linked to the low-temperature evaporator condition. The compression ratio is a measure of how severely the refrigerant vapor is compressed from the low-side evaporator pressure to the high-side condensing pressure.

Compression ratio (CR) is defined as the absolute condensing pressure (in pounds per square inch absolute (psia) divided by the absolute evaporating pressure (in psia):

CR=Pc/Pe

Where:

  • CR = compression ratio
  • Pc = absolute condensing pressure (psia)
  • Pe = absolute evaporating pressure (psia)
